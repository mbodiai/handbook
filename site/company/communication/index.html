    How We Communicate¶

    Effective communication is essential for our distributed, research-driven team. Our communication practices emphasize transparency, inclusivity, and efficient knowledge sharing.

    Communication Principles¶

    🌐 Transparency by Default¶

    • Share information openly unless there's a specific reason not to
    • Make decisions and reasoning visible to the entire team
    • Document important discussions and outcomes
    • Provide context for decisions and changes

    📝 Written First¶

    • Document important information in writing for future reference
    • Use asynchronous communication to respect different time zones and work styles
    • Create searchable records of decisions and discussions
    • Supplement verbal communication with written summaries

    🎯 Purpose-Driven¶

    • Be clear about the purpose and expected outcomes of communications
    • Choose the right medium for the message and audience
    • Respect people's time and attention
    • Follow up on action items and commitments

    🤝 Inclusive & Respectful¶

    • Ensure all team members can participate regardless of location or schedule
    • Use clear, jargon-free language when possible
    • Provide multiple ways for people to contribute and ask questions
    • Foster psychological safety for open dialogue

    Communication Channels¶

    Slack (Real-time Discussion)¶

    #general - Company-wide announcements and discussions - Important company updates - All-hands meeting coordination - General questions and discussions

    #research - Research team collaboration - Paper discussions and literature reviews - Research findings and breakthroughs - Academic collaboration opportunities

    #engineering - Engineering team coordination - Technical discussions and problem-solving - Code reviews and architecture decisions - Infrastructure updates and alerts

    #product - Product development and strategy - Customer feedback and requirements - Product roadmap discussions - Market research and competitive analysis

    #random - Casual conversation and team building - Personal updates and celebrations - Interesting articles and resources - Team building activities and events

    #alerts - System monitoring and notifications - Production system alerts - Deployment notifications - Security and compliance updates

    Email (Formal Communication)¶

    • External communication with customers, partners, and vendors
    • Legal and compliance communications
    • Formal announcements and policy changes
    • Meeting invitations and calendar coordination

    GitHub (Technical Collaboration)¶

    • Code reviews and technical discussions
    • Issue tracking and project management
    • Documentation and knowledge sharing
    • Open-source collaboration and contributions

    Notion (Documentation & Knowledge Base)¶

    • Long-form documentation and processes
    • Meeting notes and decision records
    • Project planning and tracking
    • Knowledge base and FAQ

    Video Calls (Face-to-Face Interaction)¶

    • Team meetings and standup calls
    • Customer interviews and demos
    • Brainstorming and collaborative sessions
    • One-on-one meetings and performance reviews

    Meeting Guidelines¶

    Meeting Types¶

    Standup Meetings (Daily, 15 minutes) - What did you accomplish yesterday? - What are you working on today? - Any blockers or help needed? - Keep it brief and focused on coordination

    Sprint Planning (Weekly, 1 hour) - Review completed work and lessons learned - Plan upcoming work and priorities - Identify dependencies and resource needs - Align on goals and expectations

    All-Hands (Monthly, 1 hour) - Company updates and strategic direction - Team highlights and achievements - Q&A with leadership - Recognition and celebrations

    Research Reviews (Bi-weekly, 1 hour) - Present research findings and progress - Discuss technical challenges and solutions - Plan future research directions - Collaborate on publications and presentations

    Meeting Best Practices¶

    Before the Meeting: - Create and share an agenda in advance - Include relevant background materials and context - Define clear objectives and expected outcomes - Invite only necessary participants

    During the Meeting: - Start and end on time - Follow the agenda and stay focused - Encourage participation from all attendees - Take notes and document decisions - Assign clear action items with owners and deadlines

    After the Meeting: - Share meeting notes and action items within 24 hours - Follow up on commitments and deadlines - Update relevant documentation and systems - Schedule follow-up meetings if needed

    Documentation Standards¶

    Types of Documentation¶

    Process Documentation - How to perform common tasks and procedures - Decision-making frameworks and approval processes - Onboarding and training materials - Emergency procedures and incident response

    Technical Documentation - API documentation and integration guides - Architecture decisions and design documents - Code documentation and commenting standards - Deployment and operational procedures

    Project Documentation - Project requirements and specifications - Progress updates and status reports - Lessons learned and post-mortems - Customer feedback and research findings

    Writing Guidelines¶

    Structure: - Use clear headings and organization - Include a summary or overview section - Provide step-by-step instructions where appropriate - Link to related documents and resources

    Style: - Write for your audience's knowledge level - Use active voice and clear, concise language - Include examples and visuals where helpful - Keep documents up-to-date and accurate

    Accessibility: - Use descriptive link text and alt text for images - Organize content with proper heading hierarchy - Ensure documents are searchable and discoverable - Provide multiple formats when necessary

    Remote Work Communication¶

    Time Zone Considerations¶

    • Schedule meetings during core overlap hours (10 AM - 2 PM PT)
    • Record important meetings for those who can't attend live
    • Use asynchronous communication for non-urgent matters
    • Respect local holidays and working hours

    Virtual Collaboration Tools¶

    • Use shared documents for real-time collaboration
    • Leverage screen sharing for technical discussions
    • Create virtual whiteboard sessions for brainstorming
    • Maintain social connections through virtual coffee chats

    Presence and Availability¶

    • Update your Slack status to indicate availability
    • Use calendar blocking for focused work time
    • Communicate your working hours and preferences
    • Be responsive within reasonable time frames

    Feedback Culture¶

    Giving Feedback¶

    • Be specific and actionable in your feedback
    • Focus on behaviors and outcomes, not personalities
    • Provide both positive recognition and constructive criticism
    • Choose the appropriate time and setting for feedback

    Receiving Feedback¶

    • Listen actively and ask clarifying questions
    • Separate feedback from personal identity
    • Thank people for taking the time to provide feedback
    • Follow up on action items and improvements

    Continuous Improvement¶

    • Regular team retrospectives to improve processes
    • Anonymous feedback channels for sensitive topics
    • Leadership office hours for open dialogue
    • Regular surveys to measure communication effectiveness

    Emergency Communication¶

    Incident Response¶

    • Use #alerts channel for immediate notifications
    • Follow escalation procedures for critical issues
    • Provide regular updates during ongoing incidents
    • Conduct post-incident reviews and documentation

    Crisis Communication¶

    • Designated spokespeople for external communication
    • Clear internal communication chains and responsibilities
    • Regular updates to all stakeholders
    • Coordination with legal and PR as needed

    Effective communication is a skill we continuously develop and improve. These guidelines help us work together efficiently while building strong relationships and maintaining our collaborative culture.
